Your questions answered

Everything you want to know

How big are the groups? +
Groups are intentionally small — typically 8–12 families. This ensures Dr. Monica can give meaningful attention to each dog's situation and that you build real connections with fellow participants.
What age counts as a "senior" dog? +
Generally age 7–10, depending on breed size. Large breeds age faster. If you're unsure whether your dog qualifies, apply anyway — Dr. Monica will assess in the application review.
What if my dog has a serious diagnosis? +
Glow Up is particularly powerful for families navigating illness, cancer, or chronic conditions. The end-of-life planning and quality-of-life frameworks were built precisely for these families.
How much time do I need each week? +
Plan for 60–90 minutes per week: one live group session with Dr. Monica plus time to work on your plan. All sessions are recorded if you ever need to catch up.
Is this a replacement for my vet? +
No — and that's intentional. Glow Up is coaching, not veterinary care. Dr. Monica helps you become a more informed, empowered advocate at the vet, with better questions and a clearer plan.
When does the next cohort start? +
Cohorts run a few times per year with limited spots. Apply now to be considered for the next opening — Dr. Monica will be in touch with dates once your application is reviewed.
Not sure if you're ready? +
Start with the free masterclass — "How to Slow Down Your Dog's Slowing Down." You'll get real value and a clear sense of whether Glow Up is right for where you are right now.
